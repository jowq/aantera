What is RPA

What is Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

RPA is a form of automation software which mimics actions performed by humans on a desktop with or without the need for human intervention. It’s best applied on rules-based business processes which tend to be repetitive and tedious.

RPA will achieve near universal adoption in 5 years if its trending growth continues.

Benefits

RPA is minimally invasive and implementation of RPA does not require hefty investments in shiny, next generation enterprise software nor a return on investment (ROI) graph that stretches the length of a conference room.

Simply, RPA works with existing systems by interacting with the user interface (UI). In fact, RPA is highly scalable and equally flexible that enterprises can continually optimize processes and adapt it according to the changing business environment, instead of the other way round.

A Brief History

Though the term ‘automation’ may have been coined as recent as 1946, the action of it can be traced back to Ancient Greece where Hephaestus, the god of fire and craftsmanship had self-operating robots that assisted him in the process of crafting weapons.

When Henry Ford introduce the assembly line in 1913, Ford was able to reduce time spent building the Model T from 12 hours and 8 minutes to 93 minutes and sparked a century of mass production as we know it.

It is unfortunate however that even after a century, a staggering number of business processes are still manual, reactionary with poor documentation resulting in poor time allocation and mistakes. 

78% of RPA implemented businesses will increase RPA investment significantly over the next 3 years. 

Where we are today

Recent lockdown measures and movement restriction has only exacerbate poor practices, causing decline in productivity. While attrition rate increases and output suffers, many enterprises are revisiting ways to mitigate risks, loss of knowledge and instability in a time of uncertain economic challenges.

With RPA, businesses are able to reduce cost while increasing efficiency simultaneously without further investment in human capital by automating interactions with machines. By allowing “bots” to take over, work gets done faster, mistake-free, with no breaks, round the clock.
